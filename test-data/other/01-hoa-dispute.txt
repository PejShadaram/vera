HOA DISPUTE — WESTSIDE HOMEOWNERS ASSOCIATION v. CARLOS MENDOZA
4817 Saguaro Ridge Lane, Scottsdale, AZ 85254

BACKGROUND:
Carlos Mendoza purchased property November 2022. HOA monthly dues: $285/month.
In March 2024, Carlos installed a 6-foot privacy fence along the rear property line.
He submitted plans to the HOA Architectural Review Committee (ARC) on February 12, 2024.

DISPUTE TIMELINE:

February 12, 2024:
ARC application submitted for rear privacy fence. Materials: cedar, stained dark brown. Height: 6 feet.
Application fee of $50 paid.

March 15, 2024:
Fence installation completed. Carlos had not yet received ARC approval or denial.
Carlos proceeded because the CC&Rs state ARC must respond within 30 days; 31 days had passed.

March 22, 2024:
Carlos receives letter from HOA: "Notice of Violation — Unapproved Structure."
Letter states fence was installed without approval and demands removal within 30 days.

March 28, 2024:
Carlos sends written response citing CC&Rs Article VII, Section 3: "If the ARC fails to respond within 30 days, the application shall be deemed approved by default."
HOA acknowledges receipt.

April 15, 2024:
HOA board votes to reject Carlos's "deemed approved" argument.
HOA attorney sends letter: fence must be removed or HOA will assess daily fines of $100/day starting May 1.

April 22, 2024:
Carlos requests in writing to attend the next board meeting to address the violation.

May 6, 2024:
Carlos attends board meeting. Board president states the ARC did respond — a rejection letter was allegedly sent March 8, 2024. Carlos says he never received this letter.
Carlos asks for proof of delivery. Board cannot produce delivery confirmation.
Board president: "We sent it. That's our record."

May 1 – June 30, 2024:
HOA begins assessing $100/day fines. 61 days = $6,100 in fines added to account.

July 1, 2024:
HOA places a lien on the property for $6,100 in fines plus $350 in attorney's fees = $6,450.

August 5, 2024:
Carlos sends demand letter to HOA disputing the lien. States:
1. CC&Rs Section VII deeming approval was triggered
2. HOA cannot produce proof rejection letter was delivered
3. Fines assessed without due process (no opportunity to cure before fines began)
4. Requests removal of lien and waiver of fines

August 20, 2024:
HOA attorney responds: HOA stands by its position. Offers to reduce fines by 25% ($4,838) if Carlos pays immediately.

October 1, 2024:
Carlos files complaint with Maricopa County Superior Court — Real Estate Department.
Filing fee paid: $216.
Case No. CV2024-094471

CARLOS'S POSITION:
- The fence was "deemed approved" under CC&Rs Article VII Section 3
- HOA cannot prove rejection letter was delivered
- Daily fines before any cure period violated Arizona HOA statutes and CC&Rs
- Lien is improper and should be removed
